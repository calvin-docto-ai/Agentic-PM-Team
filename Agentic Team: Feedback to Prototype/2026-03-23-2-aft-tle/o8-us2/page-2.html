How to Respond — TLE Best Practices

Your expert opinion matters. Here's what we expect from a strong TLE response:

What Makes a Good Response

Clear clinical summary Start with your assessment of the patient's condition. Example: "Patient presents with atrial fibrillation with RVR. ECG findings support intermittent SVT. Recommend rate control and further anticoagulation assessment."
Actionable recommendations Provide specific guidance the referring physician can implement. Include medication adjustments, follow-up tests, or specialist referral criteria.
Concise but complete Aim for 50–300 words. Focus on the key clinical question. Avoid excessive detail, but ensure clinical reasoning is clear.

Expected Turnaround Time

We ask that you respond within 48 hours of case receipt. Most responses are completed within 24 hours.

If you cannot respond within this window, please use the "Defer Response" option so another specialist can help the referring physician.
